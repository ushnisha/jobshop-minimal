Test 0005:

  • This test has the two demands, one for each part, both due on 2018-01-15, and both for 270 units.
  • The demand for PART001 has a priority of 1, while the demand for PART002 has a priority of 2. That is, the demand for PART001 has a higher priority and is planned first.
  • The RESOURCE_CONSTRAINED planning parameter is set to FALSE.

Variations from the Base Scenario:

  • The demand has been moved out by one day, compared to test0001. They are now due on 2018-01-15 rather than 2018-01-14.
  • Holidays are introduced on New Years Day, All Sundays, and all Second Saturdays. In this test case, the relevant dates are: 2018-01-01, 2018-01-07, 2018-01-13, 2018-01-14.
  • All other working days have 50% efficiency compared to 100%.

The expected result is as follows:

  • Both demands are satisfied on time
  • We schedule OP-10 and OP-20 for both parts at the same time on the same workcenters (resulting in workcenter overloads).
  • OP-10 and OP-20 now have a lead time of 2 days compared to 1 because of the efficiency related stretching.
  • Note that the shipment operation start similar to test0001 and the demand is met by 2018-01-14 even though it is due only on 2018-01-15. This is because we ship as soon as we complete OP-20 which cannot start on 2018-01-06 and complete on 2018-01-08 because 2018-01-07 is a holiday. Therefore OP-20 now needs to start on 2018-01-05 and complete on 2018-01-07.
  • A perfectly acceptable alternate solution is to start OP-20 on 2018-01-05, but start the SHIP operation only on 2018-01-08 to meet the demand just in time. However, at this time the solver does not support that feature.
