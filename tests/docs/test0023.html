Test 0023:

  • This test has the two demands, one for each part, both due on 2018-01-14, and both for 270 units.
  • The demand for PART001 has a priority of 1, while the demand for PART002 has a priority of 2. That is, the demand for PART001 has a higher priority and is planned first.
  • The RESOURCE_CONSTRAINED planning parameter is set to TRUE.

Variations from the Base Scenario:

  • Workcenter availability is a "hard constraint".
  • A ReleasedWorkOrder of 270 units for PART001-OP20 loads RES003 between 2018-01-05 and 2018-01-06. It is not assigned to any specific demandID.
  • Note that the ReleasedWorkOrder runs one day earlier than the JIT date (as in test0022).

The expected result is as follows:

  • Both demands are satisfied on time or early.
  • We can follow the constrained planning logic of by working through the steps of planning the demands, one at a time.
  • First we plan the SHIP for PART001 (higher priority demand and is planned first). It needs to start on 2018-01-07 to complete on time.
  • PART001-OP20 is not planned afresh; instead the solver uses the ReleasedWorkOrder completing on 2018-01-06 for the same quantity as the demand (270). As a result, we don't need to plan PART001-OP10. Since the ReleasedWorkOrder for PART001-OP20 is one day earlier than the JIT date, we also ship one day earlier and meet the demand one day earlier than due.
  • Next we plan the SHIP for PART002. It needs to start on 2018-01-07 to complete just in time. As a result OP20 for PART002 is planned to start on 2018-01-06. Note that since the WorkOrder for PART001-OP20 was planned on 2018-01-05, this date is now available for PART002-OP20. Consequently, OP10 for PART002 is also planned JIT and starts on 2018-01-05.
  • In this case, PART001 demand is delivered early (as a result of matching earlier WIP for PART001-OP20) and PART002 is delivered on time.
